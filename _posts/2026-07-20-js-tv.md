RL involves coming up with a policy over all the actions in the MDP. For LLMs, that boils down to all the tokens that can be generated, which means that the softmax of the logits is a discrete probability distribution. After every RL step, the policy gets updated but if the update is too big, it causes instabilities like [entropy collapse](https://www.jeankaddour.com/blog/entropy_collapse). Classical RL has come up with many tricks for this (e.g. [trust regions](https://spinningup.openai.com/en/latest/algorithms/trpo.html)). Here, we experiment by adding a loss penalty depending on the difference between the old and new logprobs. Our general goal is to keep training stable while not compomising on performance (i.e. benchmark score).    

Comparing probability distributions has a rich history. The most common one used in LLM training is [KL Divergence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kullback%E2%80%93Leibler_divergence), probably because of its properties and also computational complexity - modern LLM vocabulary spaces are large (around [200,000](https://huggingface.co/thinkingmachines/Inkling/blob/main/config.json#L12)) and KLD has nice unbiased and low-variance [estimators](http://joschu.net/blog/kl-approx.html).  

There're many other ways to compare probability distributions. An entire field of [optimal transport](https://www.gpeyre.com/ot4ml/myst/_build/html/) tries to do that, and Wasserstein distance has already been used earlier in neural networks, like [GANs](https://arxiv.org/abs/1701.07875). In this post, we concentrate on [Jensen–Shannon divergence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jensen%E2%80%93Shannon_divergence) and [Total Variation Distance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Total_variation_distance_of_probability_measures). Both are related to KLD. JSD is the mean of forward and reverse KLD, and TVD is related to KLD via [Pinsker's inequality](https://arxiv.org/abs/2202.07198). We would also need good estimators for both, given that it's intractable to calculate the exact metric over 200,000 tokens in every step. We can use similar to the ones for KL ([this](https://arxiv.org/abs/2607.10848) has one for TVD) using a ratio-based proxy.

### Experiment Setup

We ran the same algorithm GRPO on the GSM8K dataset, using all three estimators, adding each as a loss term. The advantage function remains unchanged. All experiments were conducted on an A100-SXM on this [commit](https://github.com/NovaSky-AI/SkyRL/commit/c78bfeed074364dea5bf4ef9553c8cb774a7bd19).

__Costs:__ Everything ran on a single A100-SXM which go for about $2 per GPU per hour. All runs took 2 hours each (=$4), so a total of $12.

### Results

We've plotted:  
1) Eval score on GSM8K  
2) Per-turn reward  
3) Policy entropy  
4) Divergence score between train and rollout policies  
5) Absolute difference mean between train and rollout policies  

Eval score and rewards are roughly the same for all three, but TVD has 50% higher entropy than the others. There's a huge spike for JSD which could be because of the reverse KL - the KLD graph corresponds to forward KL and in general, KLD goes infinite if the new policy assigns non-zero mass to a point which has zero mass in the base policy.
